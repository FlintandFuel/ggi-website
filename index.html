Common Questions

Frequently asked.

GGI works with FTSE-listed corporations, national governments, NHS trusts, Fortune 500 firms and high-growth private organisations. Engagements range from leadership programmes deployed across thousands of managers in multiple geographies, to culture transformation at board level.

The institute has delivered in 50+ countries across financial services, healthcare, government and international development. GGI's methods are designed for enterprise scale and typically involve sustained partnership, not one-off interventions.

Unwritten Ground Rules (UGRs®) are the informal norms governing how people actually behave in an organisation. Not the values on the wall. Not what employees say in a survey. What people do when no one is watching, and why.

A standard culture survey measures stated behaviour. UGRs® surfaces actual behaviour. Deployed in 100+ organisations to identify and deliberately shift the cultural norms that support or undermine strategy, through targeted leadership action, not communications campaigns.

Yes. World Class Manager is a 24-module programme designed for enterprise-scale, multi-geography deployment. CPD-accredited and formalised in a global ACCA partnership in 2024, reaching 247,000 members across 181 countries.

Programmes have been delivered across Asia, Africa, Europe, the Americas and the Middle East simultaneously. The infrastructure for global deployment already exists.

A GGI engagement starts with a strategic conversation, not a diagnostic template. The institute listens first, then designs a programme drawing on whichever methods fit the challenge: UGRs® for culture diagnosis, Growth Space™ for organisational architecture, Big Conversation for alignment at scale, World Class Manager for sustained management capability.

Outcomes are tied to business metrics from the start: strategy execution rate, engagement, leadership pipeline strength. Clients return because GGI transfers capability and builds cultures that perform long after the engagement ends.
